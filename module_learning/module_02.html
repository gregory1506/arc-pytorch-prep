Module 02 - UNet Architecture

Encoder, bottleneck, decoder, and the purpose of skip connections.

Theory highlights

  • Encoder captures context by shrinking spatial size and growing channels.
  • Decoder restores spatial detail using upsampling and skip connections.
  • Skip connections preserve localization and boundary detail.
  • Final layer outputs logits; activation happens in the loss or inference.

Flashcards

Q: Why do we need skip connections in UNet?
A: They pass fine spatial details to the decoder for accurate boundaries.
Q: What happens to spatial size in the encoder?
A: It shrinks at each level, typically by a factor of 2.
Q: Why avoid sigmoid or softmax inside the model?
A: Loss functions expect logits and handle activation more stably.
Q: Why concatenate skip features instead of adding?
A: Concatenation preserves full feature information and channel capacity.
Q: What is the minimum input size for 5 levels?
A: 256x256, so the bottleneck remains at least 8x8.

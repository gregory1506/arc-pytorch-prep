Module 04 - Metrics

IoU and Dice for segmentation quality.

Theory highlights

  • Accuracy can be misleading with imbalanced classes.
  • IoU measures overlap: intersection divided by union.
  • Dice emphasizes overlap and is smoother for optimization.
  • Smoothing prevents divide-by-zero for empty masks.

Flashcards

Q: Why is accuracy a poor metric for segmentation?
A: Background dominates the pixels, hiding poor foreground performance.
Q: How is IoU computed?
A: Intersection divided by union of prediction and ground truth.
Q: Why add smoothing to IoU or Dice?
A: To avoid divide-by-zero when both masks are empty.
Q: When do you prefer mIoU?
A: When you want to weight all classes equally.
Q: How are Dice and IoU related?
A: Dice is a monotonic transform of IoU and is always higher for the same overlap.

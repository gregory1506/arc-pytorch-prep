Module 01 - Data Pipeline

From satellite bands to normalized tiles and Dataset samples.

Theory highlights

  • Band order matters. Explicitly map B2, B3, B4, B8, B11, B12 to channels.
  • Tiling reduces memory and increases sample count for training.
  • Normalize per band using dataset statistics for stable training.
  • Rasterio reads as (bands, height, width), which matches PyTorch.

Flashcards

Q: Why is tiling necessary for Sentinel-2 scenes?
A: Full scenes are too large for memory. Tiling enables manageable batches and more samples.
Q: Why do SWIR bands help with burned area detection?
A: Burned areas have distinctive reflectance in SWIR compared to vegetation and soil.
Q: What shape should a single sample have for PyTorch?
A: (channels, height, width).
Q: Why prefer dataset statistics over per-image normalization for training?
A: It keeps scaling consistent across batches and improves convergence.
Q: How do you handle tiles at image edges?
A: Pad, drop, or use partial tiles depending on the training policy.

Module 07 - Monitoring

Metrics, logs, and health checks for production ML.

Theory highlights

  • Counters, gauges, and histograms capture different signal types.
  • Structured logs enable consistent search and alerting.
  • Liveness and readiness checks support safer deployments.
  • Watch for data drift when labels are unavailable.

Flashcards

Q: What does a histogram measure in monitoring?
A: A distribution of values like latency, enabling percentiles.
Q: Why avoid high-cardinality labels?
A: They explode metric storage and slow queries.
Q: Liveness vs readiness?
A: Liveness says the app runs; readiness says it can serve requests.
Q: How do you monitor model quality without labels?
A: Track input and output distributions and confidence drift.
Q: What should trigger an alert first?
A: Sustained error rate or latency regression beyond thresholds.

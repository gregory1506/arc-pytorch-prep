Module 03 - Training Loop

Train, validate, checkpoint, and log with AMP.

Theory highlights

  • Train mode enables dropout and batch norm updates; eval disables them.
  • AMP uses mixed precision to speed up training and reduce memory.
  • Checkpoints should include model, optimizer, scheduler, scaler, and epoch.
  • Validate per epoch for stable metrics.

Flashcards

Q: Why do we call model.train() and model.eval()?
A: It switches batch norm and dropout behavior for training vs inference.
Q: What does the GradScaler protect against?
A: Gradient underflow in mixed precision by scaling the loss.
Q: What must be saved to resume training accurately?
A: Model, optimizer, scheduler, scaler states, and current epoch.
Q: Why validate per epoch instead of per batch?
A: Validation is expensive and batch metrics are noisy.
Q: How do you sanity check a training loop?
A: Overfit a small batch until loss goes near zero.
